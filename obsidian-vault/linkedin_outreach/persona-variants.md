# LinkedIn Outreach — Persona Messaging Variants

Last updated: 2026-06-04
Maintained by: REACH (linkedin_outreach, Agent #4)

Six persona variants with distinct messaging angles.
`draft_message()` selects the variant matching `contact.persona` at runtime.

## ops_leader
**Core pain:** Manual processes and spreadsheets eating the operations team's time.
**Angle:** Efficiency and time reclaim. They own the problem and have budget authority.
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, direct, zero fluff. Skip pleasantries.
**Connect hook example:** "Noticed you're scaling ops at [company] — building on [specific tech stack] caught my eye. Happy to share what's cut reporting time for similar teams."
**NEVER:** Generic productivity claims without a named specific signal.

## revenue_leader
**Core pain:** Pipeline velocity too slow; first-touch taking hours not seconds.
**Angle:** ROI and conversion lift. They own the revenue number, not the process.
**Tone:** Lead with outcome first, then explain how. Skip the "we offer" opener.
**Connect hook example:** "Your team's growth pace at [company] stood out. We just cut a similar team's first-touch time from 4 min to 45s — worth a 15-minute chat?"
**NEVER:** Process-heavy explanations. Lead with the measurable number.

## sme_founder
**Core pain:** Wearing every hat; can't scale without another hire.
**Angle:** Leverage. They want to do more with what they already have.
**Tone:** Founder-to-founder. Acknowledge the constraint without condescension.
**Connect hook example:** "Building [company] while staying in the weeds on ops — I've been there. Found a way to reclaim 10+ hours/week without adding headcount."
**NEVER:** Enterprise language. They scroll past anything that sounds like a vendor pitch.

## agency_owner
**Core pain:** Client delivery speed squeezed by manual campaign management.
**Angle:** Delivery throughput and margin protection. Every hour saved = margin recovered.
**Tone:** Practical, outcome-focused. They care about client retention, not technology.
**NEVER:** Over-explain the automation. Lead with the client retention or margin angle.

## saas_founder
**Core pain:** Internal ops overhead consuming engineering bandwidth.
**Angle:** Technical leverage — systems thinking resonates. They appreciate architecture.
**Tone:** Systems-minded peer. Can go slightly technical without losing them.
**NEVER:** Non-technical framing. They will immediately ask "how does it work?"

## professional_services_owner
**Core pain:** Research and reporting consuming billable hours that should be client-facing.
**Angle:** Reclaiming billable capacity. Every manual hour is direct revenue loss.
**Tone:** Professional, slightly formal, compliance-aware. Emphasise control and oversight.
**NEVER:** Move too fast. They need to trust the process before they'll engage.
